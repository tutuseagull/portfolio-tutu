Launch a New Investment Product

Overview

  • Design Duration: 3.5 months (2018)
  • Client Industry: Proptech, Fintech
  • User: Accredited Investors
  • Role: Product designer in Web UX and UI design (* Collaborated with a team in user research and another designer in the web design process)
  • Services: User research, responsive web design, UX testing, marketing assets & document design

Purpose

Design and launch a new real estate investment service, and build a website to support that.

Approach

  • Study & Learn about the investment behaviors of our potential user
  • Explain a relatively new and complex investment model to the users visually and verbally
  • Provide Visual and Verbal Explanation towards new & complex investment model for users
  • Validate business models
  • Build trust between the investors and the platform through a responsive website
  • Work with existing brand DNA and create a new style guide for the targeted visitors

The Moments

Engaging and building trust with users

Within a 3-month timeframe, I and another designer together constructed a website with the product and marketing team. During the first month, I have worked closely with the product team on user research, competitor research, business model validation, and end-to-end experience. After the research phase, a copywriter joined and we were able to fast-paced the development of contents, architecture, a new style guide, and the final website design and iterations.

The starting point and the aims were

  • Launching an online real estate investment service which takes care of all the rental management efforts, so that the investor can invest in remote properties with less work.
  • Targeting a more specific group of users, the accredited investor
  • Creating a new website, which would be the main touchpoint leading deal closure or further contact with the sales team. The measurements of success were selling out all the listing properties, and having 500+ signups on the waitlist.

Design Principle: Credibility > Simplicity

When working for clients in many industries, the common goal is to create an intuitive and simple experience for the vast majority of users. However, one important experience I have learned from this project is, in some industries, such as financial and healthcare, a “smooth UX flow” is usually prioritized instead of “Credibility” during decision-making.

In this case, we had to communicate information accurately and clearly to build trust, and lead to closing deals. Additionally, we wanted the visitors to feel exclusive to the offerings.

Discovery Interviews

Since the targeted users are accredited investors, I conducted some interviews with investors who had the same qualifications as the accredited investors, previous customers from the client company, and registered investment advisors (RIA).

(3 key values that got the users excited)

Key learnings that guided the design decisions:

  1. They liked the concept of a hassle-free investment process for saving their time and energy.
  2. Investors asked a lot of questions before diving into the listing. This pushed us to develop the CTA “Browse Properties” a few steps later in the user journey; and we also decided to make the website more informative.
  3. Potential buyers are often experts in investment but not necessarily in real estate, so they rely on us to provide more context.
  4. Investors tend to be more excited when looking at the comparison of investment models with specific numeric data.
  5. Transparency to all the costs was always appreciated.

In addition, we made a decision on the investment model and finalized the end-to-end user journey after listening to the interview feedback on different options.

Information Architecture and Design Messaging

With the help of a copywriter and lessons learned from interviews & some wireframe testing, we were able to finalize the content strategy for the website in time. Once the strategy was developed, the next challenge was to make our website informative, supported by reliable data sources, and efficient enough to engage with the investors.

At the same time, more marketing messages were brought to the table as part of the design decision:

  • The first group of investors was exclusively invited.
  • The service was powered by AI to generate predictive data analysis.
  • A huge amount of information will be broken down into different levels in the website architecture for investors.
  • We wanted to introduce a new and sophisticated look and feel on the website, meanwhile, inherited some DNA from the existing brand guideline.

Iterating the Contents

Once the goals and website architecture were clear, it then allowed us to focus on the brand messaging and details.

(Testing messaging on the homepage)

Iterating the VI and Design System

Based on our persona and interview insights, I did some thorough research on the visual language of premium services/products and their everyday go-to website, from luxurious airlines, investment platforms, to Financial TImes and etc. This really helped us to analyze which color palettes and typography is considered more SOPHISTICATED, PREMIUM, and HI-TECH.

Then we did quick mockups with different looks and themes and selected the design direction among the team.

We decided to keep the signature orange from the original company brand, but apply a darker navy theme with “One Serif Font.”

In addition, we have also explored how to visually translate value propositions and marketing messages through many iterations. One example would be how the properties were carefully hand-picked by the client, and how technology powered the process.
